// about

I'm a first-year BCA student trying to understand how software actually works — not just how to write it. Most of my time goes into reading about operating systems, poking around Linux, and building small things that solve real problems.

Backend engineering makes sense to me. I like the idea of systems that do work quietly and reliably — things that don't need to be flashy to be good. I'm drawn to infrastructure, how servers talk to each other, and how software handles load and failure.

On the side, I've been curious about UAV technology — autonomous systems that blend software with the physical world. It's early interest, but it's a direction I keep coming back to.

Right now I'm focused on building fundamentals. Writing Python. Learning C++. Reading about data structures. Trying not to skip steps.
